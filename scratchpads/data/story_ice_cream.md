### Broken consequentialism

Later, @m_ashcroft continues a year later with this tweet (part of a longer thread on the same topic):

---

**@m_ashcroft**  
*2022-09-27*

> Once again thinking back to when I noticed some part of me that was convinced it needed to 'do' the world, as if it had taken on responsibility and burden for the ongoing existence of everything
>
> And it was using muscle tension to do it
>
> When it let go everything got more vivid

---

**@m_ashcroft**  
*2022-09-27*

> I suspect this pattern is probably quite common, just going on below conscious awareness
>
> probably an example of inappropriate learning of cause and effect in childhood
>
> "I wanted to X happen, and when I happened to be doing Y, X happened, so Y must cause X. I must always Y."

---

The problem is broken consequentialist thinking. Tensing your body has always been used to bring about the thing desired, so it becomes reinforced over and over as the mechanism through which things get obtained in the world. Bringing tons of unnecessary body tension and numbness as an adult.

This pattern comes from an overgeneralization of a genuine pattern. We tend to manipulate the world by acting and moving, tensing our muscles to interact with tools and achieve goals. So our inner world is wired to think of "getting a certain outcome" through the contraction of muscles.
